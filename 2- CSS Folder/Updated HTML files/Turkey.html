Shipping From And To Turkey


The Port of Istanbul is also called the Port of Haydarpaşa (code TRHAY). The port authority is the General Directorate of Turkish State Railways (TCDD). TCDD owns a total of seven ports in Turkey and has connections to two more ports. The Port of Istanbul is one of the three major Turkey’s shipping ports, along with the Port of Ambarlı, and the Port of Zeytinburnu. As it was surpassed in TEUs by the Port of Ambarlı. Now, it is the third-largest port in Turkey. Haydarpaşa’s annual cargo volume exceeds 60 million tons. The Port of Haydarpaşa is located at the southern entrance to the Bosphorus.












The Port of Mersin s a major seaport located on the north-eastern coast of Mediterranean Sea in Mersin, southern Turkey. As one of the largest harbors in the country, it is Turkey's main gateway to the Mediterranean Sea. It was constructed during the 1950s as a major government project. It is the country's second largest port after Ambarli, near Istanbul.
